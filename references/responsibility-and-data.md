# Responsibility, ethics and data protection standard

Shared standard for every skill in this repo. Core rule: protection comes from mechanisms, not from a note at the end. The line "this is not legal advice" stops no error and transfers no responsibility.

## Five layers of protection (before any disclaimer)

1. **Source verification** - provisions and case law come from databases, not the model's memory.
2. **Confidence class** - every legal claim marked verified / check / do not use (see `citation-style.md`).
3. **Premise check** - facts supplied by the user are tested before analysis, not taken at face value.
4. **Explicit negative scope** - each skill states what it does NOT do and where its competence ends.
5. **Human gate** - a qualified person reviews and approves the output and carries professional responsibility.

If an error would pass without being stopped by one of layers 1-5, the fault is in the skill. Fix the tool, do not add a note.

## Human gate

Nothing is sent, filed, signed, or published before a qualified person checks and approves it. Irreversible, outward-facing acts stay with the human - the tool prepares a draft, it does not act.

## Every output is a draft

Skill outputs are AI-generated drafts. When you pass one on, mark it as such. The transparency duty follows from the AI Act (Art. 50) - the recipient must know they are working with an AI product.

## Personal data

- **Local anonymisation** - replace personal data with placeholders locally, before it reaches the model. Personal data should not leave for the API. This is data minimisation.
- **Processor agreement** - if personal data is processed in an external tool or the cloud, a data processing agreement with the provider is required (GDPR Art. 28). Check retention and whether data is transferred outside the EEA.
- **Sensitive data and professional secrecy** - before bringing in a document, assess separately whether privileged or particularly sensitive material may enter the tool at all. When in doubt, do not transfer.

## What this standard does NOT do

- It does not detect conflicts of interest between engagements - that is the lawyer's responsibility.
- It does not grant a right of representation - a skill does not make an unqualified person an attorney.
- It does not replace legal advice, especially for non-lawyers.
- It does not decide whether confidential data may enter the system - a human makes that call.
